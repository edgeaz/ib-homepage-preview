PCOS Treatment in Scottsdale, AZ

Polycystic ovary syndrome touches cycles, skin, energy, mood and metabolism all at once. We take the whole picture seriously — starting with a careful evaluation and building a plan around your health and your goals.

Book a ConsultationPatient Portal

Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) is one of the most common hormone-related conditions, yet it can be surprisingly hard to name. It affects the menstrual cycle, but its ripple effects reach skin, hair, weight, mood and long-term metabolic health. At Internally Balanced, we look at how those pieces fit together rather than treating a single symptom in isolation.

Our approach is collaborative and unhurried. We listen to your story, review your history and consider how your symptoms connect, so any care we offer is grounded in your individual picture — not a one-size-fits-all template.

Common reasons people seek evaluation

Every person is different, but people often ask about a PCOS evaluation when they notice patterns such as:

  • Irregular, infrequent, absent or unpredictable menstrual cycles
  • Difficulty with weight or unexplained weight changes despite effort
  • Acne, oily skin or other skin changes
  • Unwanted hair growth or thinning hair on the scalp
  • Persistent fatigue, low energy or brain fog
  • Changes in mood, irritability or low motivation
  • Questions about fertility or family planning
  • Cravings, energy dips or concerns about blood sugar and metabolism
These lists are educational and general. Many symptoms overlap with other conditions, which is exactly why an individual evaluation matters.

Who this may be appropriate for

PCOS-focused care may be appropriate for those with a prior diagnosis, those with symptoms that suggest PCOS but no formal diagnosis, and those who simply want a clearer understanding of what is happening in their bodies. It may not be appropriate for everyone, and that is part of what an evaluation helps determine. Some symptoms attributed to PCOS have other explanations, and if a different path is a better fit for you, we will say so.

How evaluation works

Evaluation begins with a thorough conversation. We review your medical and family history, cycle patterns, current symptoms, lifestyle, stressors and prior care. From there, appropriate testing may be recommended to help clarify the picture and rule out conditions that can look similar. Any testing is chosen based on your presentation and reviewed together, so you understand what is being looked at and why. Rather than reacting to isolated results, we interpret findings in the context of how you actually feel.

Personalized treatment options

There is no single plan that fits everyone with PCOS. Depending on your evaluation, care may involve lifestyle, nutritional and metabolic support, targeted follow-up, and — where appropriate and individualized — other therapies discussed openly with you. Any plan is tailored to your goals, whether those center on symptoms, cycle regularity, metabolic health or family planning, and we review the reasoning, alternatives and what to expect. We do not prescribe from a script, and we adjust over time as your needs evolve.

Benefits & what to expect

Many people pursue PCOS care hoping to better understand their bodies and to feel steadier — more predictable cycles, clearer skin, more consistent energy and a sense of being heard. When care is well matched, these are the kinds of improvements people often describe. We share what is realistic for your situation and set expectations honestly. Because every person responds differently, we focus on your individual progress rather than guaranteed outcomes.

Safety, monitoring & limitations

PCOS care is a medical decision that deserves care and candor. PCOS is generally managed over time rather than cured, not every option is right for every person, and results vary. When a plan is started, ongoing monitoring and follow-up are part of the process so it can be reviewed and adjusted safely. Because PCOS can affect long-term metabolic and reproductive health, we will always discuss the limitations and alternatives so you can make an informed choice.

Frequently asked questions

Is PCOS something that can be evaluated even without a prior diagnosis?

Yes. Many people come in with symptoms but no formal diagnosis. An evaluation looks at your history, symptoms and appropriate testing to help clarify what is going on and whether PCOS or another condition fits your picture.

Do I need a referral to be seen for PCOS concerns?

No referral is required to request a consultation. You can book online or call the office at 480‑863‑8778 to get started.

Will I need ongoing follow-up for PCOS?

Often, yes. PCOS is typically managed over time, so follow-up visits and appropriate monitoring help your plan be reviewed and adjusted as your needs change.

Can PCOS be cured?

PCOS is generally considered a chronic condition that is managed rather than cured. The goal of care is to understand your individual picture and support your symptoms and health over time. Results vary from person to person.

Women's Hormone Therapy in Scottsdale, AZ

Thoughtful, individualized care for the shifts in energy, mood, sleep and cycle that so often get dismissed. We start by understanding you — then build a plan around your health and your goals.

Book a ConsultationPatient Portal

Hormones influence far more than reproduction — they shape your energy, sleep, mood, metabolism and how you feel day to day. When they fall out of balance, the effects can be easy to notice but hard to name. At Internally Balanced, we take those concerns seriously and look for the underlying reasons rather than treating a single number in isolation.

Our approach is collaborative and unhurried. We listen to your story, review your history and consider how your symptoms fit together, so any care we offer is grounded in your individual picture — not a one-size-fits-all template.

Common reasons people seek evaluation

Every person is different, but women often ask about a hormonal evaluation when they notice patterns such as:

  • Persistent fatigue or low energy that rest does not resolve
  • Changes in mood, irritability, anxiousness or low motivation
  • Difficulty falling or staying asleep
  • Irregular, heavier, lighter or unpredictable menstrual cycles
  • Changes in libido or sexual comfort
  • Unexplained weight changes or difficulty with weight despite effort
  • Brain fog or trouble concentrating
  • Changes in skin, hair or overall sense of well-being
These lists are educational and general. Many symptoms overlap with other conditions, which is exactly why an individual evaluation matters.

Who this may be appropriate for

Women's hormone care may be appropriate for those experiencing symptoms that interfere with daily life, those navigating natural transitions across the reproductive years, and those who simply want a clearer understanding of what is happening in their bodies. It may not be appropriate for everyone, and that is part of what an evaluation helps determine. If a different path is a better fit for you, we will say so.

How evaluation works

Evaluation begins with a thorough conversation. We review your medical and family history, current symptoms, lifestyle, stressors, sleep and prior care. From there, appropriate testing may be recommended to help clarify the picture. Any testing is chosen based on your presentation and reviewed together, so you understand what is being looked at and why. Rather than reacting to isolated results, we interpret findings in the context of how you actually feel.

Personalized treatment options

There is no single plan that fits everyone. Depending on your evaluation, care may involve lifestyle and nutritional support, targeted follow-up, and — where appropriate and individualized — hormone-related therapy. Any plan is tailored to you and discussed openly, including the reasoning, alternatives and what to expect. We do not prescribe from a script, and we adjust over time as your needs evolve.

Benefits & what to expect

Many people pursue hormone-related care hoping to feel more like themselves — steadier energy, better sleep, clearer thinking and a sense of balance. When care is well matched, these are the kinds of improvements people often describe. We share what is realistic for your situation and set expectations honestly. Because every person responds differently, we focus on your individual progress rather than guaranteed outcomes.

Safety, monitoring & limitations

Hormone-related care is a medical decision that deserves care and candor. Not everyone is a candidate, some options carry risks or considerations that we review with you, and results vary. When a plan is started, ongoing monitoring and follow-up are part of the process so it can be reviewed and adjusted safely over time. We will always discuss the limitations and alternatives so you can make an informed choice.

Frequently asked questions

Is women's hormone therapy right for me?

That depends on your symptoms, health history and goals. The first step is an evaluation so a plan, if any, can be individualized to you. Therapy is not appropriate for everyone.

Do I need a referral to be seen?

No referral is required to request a consultation. You can book online or call the office at 480‑863‑8778 to get started.

How long before I might notice a difference?

Every person responds differently, and timelines vary. We set realistic expectations at your visit and monitor your response over time rather than promising a specific result.

Will I need ongoing follow-up?

Yes. Hormone-related care involves follow-up visits and appropriate monitoring so your plan can be reviewed and adjusted as your needs change.

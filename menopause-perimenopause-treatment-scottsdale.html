Menopause & Perimenopause Treatment in Scottsdale, AZ

The transition years can bring real, disruptive symptoms — and they deserve real attention. We help you understand what is changing and build a plan that fits your body, your stage and your goals.

Book a ConsultationPatient Portal

Perimenopause and menopause are natural transitions, but that does not mean their symptoms should be brushed aside. As hormone levels shift, many women experience changes that affect sleep, mood, temperature, energy and quality of life. At Internally Balanced, we meet you where you are — listening carefully and looking at the whole picture rather than dismissing your concerns as "just your age."

Our goal is to help you feel informed and supported. We take time to explain what is happening physiologically and to build care around your individual stage and needs, whether you are in early perimenopause or well past your final period.

Common reasons people seek evaluation

Symptoms vary widely from person to person, but women often reach out when they notice changes such as:

  • Hot flashes or night sweats
  • Irregular, skipped or unpredictable menstrual cycles
  • Difficulty sleeping or waking frequently
  • Mood changes, irritability or increased anxiousness
  • Brain fog, forgetfulness or trouble concentrating
  • Vaginal dryness or changes in comfort and intimacy
  • Fatigue and reduced energy
  • Changes in weight or how your body responds to your usual routine
These lists are educational and general. Symptoms overlap with other conditions, so an individual evaluation is the best way to understand what is driving how you feel.

Who this may be appropriate for

This care may be appropriate for women noticing early perimenopausal changes, those in the thick of the transition, and those who have reached menopause and want relief or reassurance. It may also suit those who tried to "wait it out" and found their symptoms persisting. An evaluation helps determine what stage you are in and whether treatment is appropriate for you — and if a different approach fits better, we will tell you.

How evaluation works

We begin with an in-depth conversation about your cycle history, symptoms, medical and family history, lifestyle and priorities. Where helpful, appropriate testing may be recommended to clarify your hormonal picture and rule out other contributors. Testing is selected based on your presentation and reviewed together, so decisions are made with — not for — you.

Personalized treatment options

There is no universal prescription for the menopause transition. Depending on your evaluation, care may include lifestyle and nutritional support, symptom-focused strategies, and — where appropriate and individualized — hormone-related therapy. Every plan is tailored to your stage, symptoms and preferences, and we discuss the reasoning, options and trade-offs so you can choose with confidence. Plans are revisited and adjusted over time.

Benefits & what to expect

Many women seek care hoping for better sleep, steadier moods, fewer disruptive symptoms and a renewed sense of themselves. When care is well matched to your needs, these are the kinds of improvements people often describe. We set expectations honestly and focus on your individual response rather than promising specific results, because every person moves through this transition differently.

Safety, monitoring & limitations

Menopause care involves real medical decisions. Not every option is right for every person, some carry risks or considerations we review with you, and outcomes vary. When a plan is started, ongoing monitoring and follow-up are part of the process so it can be evaluated and adjusted safely. We are candid about limitations and alternatives so your choices are truly informed.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between perimenopause and menopause?

Perimenopause is the transition leading up to menopause, when hormone levels fluctuate and symptoms may begin. Menopause is generally marked after twelve consecutive months without a menstrual period. Symptoms and needs can differ between these stages.

Do I have to have stopped my period to be seen?

No. Many people seek help during perimenopause, while cycles are still occurring but changing. An evaluation can be helpful at any point in the transition.

Is hormone therapy the only option?

No. Care is individualized and may include lifestyle, nutritional and other supportive approaches. Any hormone-related therapy is one possible option that is discussed and tailored to you, and it is not appropriate for everyone.

Will I need ongoing follow-up?

Yes. Care through the menopause transition involves follow-up and appropriate monitoring so your plan can be reviewed and adjusted as your symptoms and needs change.

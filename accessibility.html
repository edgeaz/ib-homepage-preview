Accessibility Statement

We want everyone to be able to use our website and access our care. Here is our commitment to accessibility and how to reach us if something isn't working for you.

Book a ConsultationPatient Portal

Internally Balanced is committed to ensuring that our website is accessible to people with disabilities. We believe everyone deserves an equal opportunity to access information about their health and our services, and we work to make our digital experience welcoming to all.

Last updated: July 16, 2026. Effective upon publication following practice review.

Our commitment

We strive to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 at Level AA, published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible to people with a wide range of disabilities, including visual, auditory, physical, speech, cognitive and neurological disabilities. We treat accessibility as an ongoing effort rather than a one-time task.

Measures we take

To support an accessible experience, we work to:

  • Provide text alternatives for meaningful images
  • Use clear headings and a logical, consistent page structure
  • Support keyboard navigation and a visible "skip to content" link
  • Aim for sufficient color contrast and readable text sizing
  • Label interactive elements and forms so they work with assistive technologies
  • Design responsive layouts that adapt to different devices and screen sizes

Ongoing improvement

Accessibility is a continual process. We periodically review our website and work to identify and address barriers as technology, standards and our content evolve. Some content or third-party components may not yet fully meet our target level, and we welcome feedback that helps us prioritize improvements.

Third-party content

Some features of our site link to services operated by other companies, such as our Patient Portal and Fullscript dispensary. We do not control the accessibility of third-party websites, but we are happy to help you find the information or assistance you need — just contact us.

How to report an accessibility issue

If you encounter any difficulty using our website, or need information in an alternative format, please let us know. We will make reasonable efforts to assist you and to address the issue. When you contact us, it helps to include the page or feature involved and a description of the problem.

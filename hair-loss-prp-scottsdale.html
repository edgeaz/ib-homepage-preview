Hair Loss & PRP in Scottsdale, AZ

Hair changes can be frustrating and personal, and they often have more than one cause. We start by understanding what may be driving yours — then discuss sensible, individualized options in plain language.

Book a ConsultationPatient Portal

Thinning hair or increased shedding can be unsettling, and it rarely comes down to a single factor. Hormones, nutrition, thyroid function, stress, medical conditions and genetics can all play a role. At Internally Balanced, we approach hair concerns the same way we approach the rest of your health — by looking for the underlying reasons rather than jumping to a one-size-fits-all answer.

Our approach is collaborative and unhurried. We listen to your story, review your history and consider how your concerns fit together, so any guidance we offer is grounded in your individual picture.

Common reasons people seek an evaluation

People often ask about a hair evaluation when they notice patterns such as:

  • Increased shedding or hair coming out more than usual
  • Gradual thinning across the scalp or in specific areas
  • Changes in hair texture, density or growth
  • Hair changes alongside fatigue, stress or hormonal shifts
  • Concerns that appeared after illness, life changes or major stress
  • A wish to understand whether an underlying cause is contributing
These lists are educational and general. Many symptoms overlap with other conditions, which is exactly why an individual evaluation matters.

Who this may be appropriate for

A hair loss evaluation may be appropriate for those noticing thinning or shedding, those who want to understand a possible underlying cause, and those exploring whether options such as PRP may be suitable for them. It may not be appropriate for everyone, and that is part of what an evaluation helps determine. If a different path is a better fit for you, we will say so.

How the consultation works

Evaluation begins with a thorough conversation. We review your medical and family history, current symptoms, nutrition, stress, thyroid and hormonal considerations, and any prior care. From there, appropriate testing may be recommended to help clarify the picture. Any testing is chosen based on your presentation and reviewed together, so you understand what is being looked at and why. Understanding the likely contributors comes before any discussion of options.

Understanding PRP (general information)

PRP stands for platelet-rich plasma. In general terms, it is a preparation made from a small sample of your own blood that concentrates certain components of the plasma. PRP is used in a variety of medical settings, and it is one topic some people ask about in the context of hair concerns. This information is educational only. Whether PRP or any other option is appropriate for you is a clinical decision that depends on your evaluation, your health history and your goals, and it is discussed individually rather than assumed.

Benefits & what to expect

People exploring hair concerns understandably hope to feel more confident and informed. What we can offer is a careful evaluation, plain-language explanation of the likely contributors, and an honest conversation about reasonable options. We do not promise regrowth or specific results, because hair loss has many causes and responses vary from person to person. We focus on understanding your situation and setting realistic expectations together.

Safety, monitoring & limitations

Any decision related to hair care deserves candor. Not everyone is a candidate for every option, some options carry considerations or risks that we would review with you, and outcomes vary and cannot be guaranteed. Some causes of hair loss need specific medical evaluation, and we will refer or coordinate when that is the right step. When any plan is pursued, appropriate follow-up is part of the process so it can be reviewed over time.

Frequently asked questions

What is PRP?

PRP stands for platelet-rich plasma, a preparation made from a small sample of your own blood. It is used in various medical settings. Whether it is appropriate for you is something to discuss during an individual evaluation.

Do I need a referral to be seen?

No referral is required to request a consultation. You can book online or call the office at 480‑863‑8778 to get started.

Will treatment regrow my hair?

We do not promise regrowth or specific results. Hair loss has many causes, responses vary from person to person, and an evaluation is the first step to understand what may be appropriate for you.

Why does an evaluation come first?

Hair loss can stem from many factors, including hormones, nutrition, stress, thyroid function and genetics. Understanding the underlying cause helps guide a sensible, individualized plan.

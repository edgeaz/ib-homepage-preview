Medical Weight Loss in Scottsdale, AZ

Weight is rarely about willpower alone, hormones, metabolism, sleep and stress all play a part. We start by understanding what's driving your experience, then build a provider-guided plan around your health and your goals.

Book a ConsultationPatient Portal

Weight is shaped by far more than diet and effort. Hormones, metabolism, sleep, stress, medications and underlying health conditions can all influence how your body holds and releases weight. When those factors are overlooked, well-intentioned efforts can stall, which is discouraging and, too often, unfairly blamed on the person. At Internally Balanced, we take weight seriously and look for the underlying reasons rather than handing out a generic plan.

Our approach is collaborative and unhurried. We listen to your story, review your history and consider how your symptoms and goals fit together, so any care we offer is grounded in your individual picture, not a one-size-fits-all template.

Common reasons people seek evaluation

Every person is different, but people often ask about a medical weight-loss evaluation when they notice patterns such as:

  • Difficulty losing weight despite consistent diet and exercise
  • Weight changes that seem out of step with lifestyle
  • Persistent fatigue, low energy or poor sleep
  • Strong cravings, appetite changes or energy dips through the day
  • Weight concerns connected to hormones, thyroid or PCOS
  • Changes in mood, motivation or stress affecting daily habits
  • Concerns about metabolic health, such as blood sugar or energy balance
  • A desire for provider-guided support rather than another fad diet
These lists are educational and general. Many symptoms overlap with other conditions, which is exactly why an individual evaluation matters.

Who this may be appropriate for

Medical weight-loss care may be appropriate for those who have struggled to reach their goals on their own, those whose weight may be connected to hormonal or metabolic factors, and those who simply want a clearer, provider-guided understanding of what is happening in their bodies. It may not be appropriate for everyone, and that is part of what an evaluation helps determine. If a different path is a better fit for you, we will say so.

How evaluation works

Evaluation begins with a thorough conversation. We review your medical and family history, current symptoms, lifestyle, nutrition, activity, sleep, stressors and prior efforts. From there, appropriate testing may be recommended to help clarify contributing factors such as hormonal or metabolic influences. Any testing is chosen based on your presentation and reviewed together, so you understand what is being looked at and why. Rather than reacting to isolated results, we interpret findings in the context of your goals and how you actually feel.

Personalized treatment options

There is no single plan that fits everyone. Depending on your evaluation, care may involve nutritional and lifestyle support, help with sleep and stress, attention to hormonal or metabolic contributors, targeted follow-up, and, where appropriate and individualized, other therapies discussed openly with you. Any plan is tailored to you and reviewed together, including the reasoning, alternatives and what to expect. We do not prescribe from a script, and we adjust over time as your needs evolve.

Benefits & what to expect

Many people pursue medical weight-loss care hoping for more than a number on a scale, steadier energy, better sleep, healthier habits and a sense of working with a provider who takes their concerns seriously. When care is well matched, these are the kinds of improvements people often describe. We share what is realistic for your situation and set expectations honestly. Because every person responds differently, we focus on your individual progress and overall health rather than guaranteed outcomes.

Safety, monitoring & limitations

Weight-related care is a medical decision that deserves care and candor. Not everyone is a candidate for every approach, some options carry risks or considerations that we review with you, and results vary. When a plan is started, ongoing monitoring and follow-up are part of the process so it can be reviewed and adjusted safely over time. We will always discuss the limitations and alternatives so you can make an informed choice.

Frequently asked questions

How is medical weight loss different from a diet program?

Medical weight loss is guided by a provider and grounded in an evaluation of your health, history and goals, rather than a generic plan. The aim is an individualized, monitored approach that considers the factors influencing your weight.

Do I need a referral to be seen?

No referral is required to request a consultation. You can book online or call the office at 480‑863‑8778 to get started.

Will I need ongoing follow-up?

Yes. Weight-related care involves follow-up visits and appropriate monitoring so your plan can be reviewed and adjusted as your needs change.

How much weight will I lose and how fast?

Everyone responds differently, and we do not promise a specific amount or timeline. We set realistic expectations at your visit and focus on your individual progress and health over time.
